# Abuse Cost Model - Knowledge Sharing Community

## Overview

This document defines the comprehensive abuse cost model that quantifies, measures, and manages the economic and social costs of abuse within the Knowledge Sharing Community. The model establishes mechanisms to prevent, detect, and mitigate abuse while creating economic incentives for positive behavior and sustainable community health.

## Abuse Cost Framework

### Core Economic Principles

#### 1. Cost Internalization
**External Cost Internalization:** Abuse costs are borne by perpetrators
**Social Cost Accounting:** All social costs are measured and accounted
**Economic Efficiency:** Resources allocated efficiently to prevent abuse
**Market Mechanisms:** Market forces discourage abusive behavior

#### 2. Behavioral Economics
**Deterrence Theory:** High costs deter abusive behavior
**Loss Aversion:** Potential losses more motivating than gains
**Social Norms:** Community standards influence behavior
**Reputation Economics:** Reputation loss creates economic costs

#### 3. Risk Management
**Risk Assessment:** Abuse risks identified and quantified
**Risk Mitigation:** Strategies implemented to reduce abuse
**Risk Transfer:** Insurance and protection mechanisms
**Risk Monitoring:** Continuous abuse risk monitoring
