# Reputation Economics - Knowledge Sharing Community

## Overview

This document defines the comprehensive reputation economics model that treats reputation as a measurable, tradable, and accumulative asset within the Knowledge Sharing Community. The model establishes reputation as economic capital that drives value creation, opportunity access, and sustainable growth.

## Reputation Economic Framework

### Core Economic Principles

#### 1. Reputation as Capital Asset
**Scarcity Principle:** High reputation is scarce and valuable
**Utility Principle:** Reputation provides tangible benefits and opportunities
**Exchange Principle:** Reputation can be exchanged for value
**Accumulation Principle:** Reputation builds and compounds over time

#### 2. Market Dynamics
**Supply and Demand:** Reputation supply is limited, demand is high
**Price Mechanism:** Reputation value determined by market forces
**Competition:** Users compete for reputation through quality contributions
**Equilibrium:** Market reaches balance through reputation economics

#### 3. Behavioral Economics
**Signaling Theory:** Reputation signals quality and trustworthiness
**Social Proof:** High reputation influences others' behavior
**Loss Aversion:** Reputation loss feared more than gain valued
**Reciprocity Norm:** Reputation sharing creates reciprocal benefits
