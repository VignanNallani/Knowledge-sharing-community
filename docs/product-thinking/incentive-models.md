# Incentive Models - Knowledge Sharing Community

## Overview

This document outlines comprehensive incentive models designed to drive desired behaviors across all stakeholder groups in the Knowledge Sharing Community platform. The models balance intrinsic and extrinsic motivators while maintaining platform health and sustainability.

## Incentive Design Principles

### Core Principles
1. **Alignment:** Incentives must align with platform mission and values
2. **Sustainability:** Incentives must be economically and operationally sustainable
3. **Fairness:** Systems must be transparent and perceived as fair
4. **Scalability:** Models must work at platform scale
5. **Quality Focus:** Incentives must prioritize quality over quantity
6. **Trust Building:** Systems must enhance rather than undermine trust

### Behavioral Economics Foundations
- **Loss Aversion:** People fear losing more than they value gaining
- **Social Proof:** People follow behaviors of others they trust
- **Reciprocity:** People feel obligated to return favors
- **Status Seeking:** People value recognition and social standing
- **Mastery Motivation:** People enjoy developing competence

## Creator Incentive Models

### 1. Reputation-Based Incentives

#### Expert Status System
**Mechanism:** Tiered expertise levels with increasing privileges
- **Bronze Contributor:** 0-100 quality points
- **Silver Contributor:** 101-500 quality points
- **Gold Contributor:** 501-2000 quality points
- **Platinum Expert:** 2001-5000 quality points
- **Diamond Master:** 5000+ quality points

**Benefits per Tier:**
- Visibility boost in content feeds
- Access to exclusive collaboration opportunities
- Enhanced moderation privileges
- Direct access to platform leadership
- Speaking opportunities at events

#### Quality Score Calculation
```
Quality Score = (Content Quality × 0.4) + 
                (Community Impact × 0.3) + 
                (Peer Recognition × 0.2) + 
                (Mentorship Activity × 0.1)
```
